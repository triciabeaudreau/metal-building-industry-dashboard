# 🏗️ Metal Building Industry Intelligence Dashboard

**Tools:** Power BI, Excel, Claude AI (Anthropic), Tableau  
**Year:** 2026

## Project Overview
A 5-page client-facing intelligence dashboard analyzing the U.S. metal building 
industry, covering market growth, steel pricing and tariff impact, AI adoption 
trends, HubSpot CRM opportunity analysis, and industry outlook. Data was sourced 
and structured using AI-assisted research from publicly available industry reports.

## Dashboard Pages

**Page 1 — Market Overview & Growth**
- U.S. market size: $12.98B (2024) → $27.10B (2033), 8.6% CAGR
- Market share by segment: Manufacturing (34%), Warehouse/Distribution (24%), 
Commercial/Retail (16%), Agricultural (12%), Other (14%)

**Page 2 — U.S. Demand by State**
- Estimated demand index by state based on market share, regional growth trends, 
and construction activity
- Interactive Tableau map: [View Here](https://public.tableau.com/app/profile/patricia.beaudreau/viz/MetalBuildingDemand/Sheet1)

**Page 3 — Steel Pricing & Tariff Impact**
- HRC steel price history 2018–2026: peaked at $1,800/ton (2021), returned to 
~$950/ton (2024), 47% drop from peak
- Section 232 tariff raised to 50% in June 2025
- Building cost by type (kit vs. installed cost per sq/ft)

**Page 4 — AI in the Metal Building Industry**
- AI construction spending forecast: $4.96B (2025) → $14.72B (2030)
- AI use case analysis mapped to industry relevance: lead scoring, smart quoting, 
predictive maintenance, supply chain AI, BIM integration

**Page 5 — HubSpot AI-Powered Sales Opportunity**
- CRM adoption analysis identifying 7 HubSpot AI features with estimated 29% 
sales lift and 34% customer retention improvement
- AI-powered sales pipeline model showing impact at each stage from inbound 
leads through closed won

## Key Findings
- U.S. metal building market projected to more than double by 2033 driven by 
e-commerce expansion, manufacturing reshoring, and prefab adoption
- Manufacturing is the fastest growing segment at 9.6% CAGR (2025–2033)
- Steel prices have stabilized near pre-pandemic levels but Section 232 tariff 
volatility remains a high-impact risk
- AI adoption in construction projected to nearly triple by 2030 — early movers 
have a significant competitive advantage
- HubSpot AI implementation could save an estimated 5 hours of admin work per 
week while improving close rates
